Project: Poster Design Using Text, Shapes, and Filters

Course Catalog Day-wise Breakdown

Introduction to the Poster Design Project

In this project, you will create a visually appealing poster using Adobe Photoshop. This lesson will guide you through utilizing text, shapes, and filters to enhance your design.

Getting Started with Your Poster

1. Setting Up Your Document

To start, create a new document with the following specifications:

  • Size: 24 x 36 inches
  • Resolution: 300 DPI
  • Color Mode: RGB Color

2. Choosing a Theme

Decide on a theme for your poster. Consider the following:

  • Event or purpose of the poster (e.g., concert, promotion, informational).
  • Color scheme and visual style.

Creating Your Poster Design

3. Adding Background and Shapes

Start by adding a background color or image to your poster:

  • Select the Paint Bucket Tool or use the File > Place Embedded option for images.
  • Create shapes using the Shape Tool to add visual interest.
  • Experiment with layer styles to add depth (e.g., shadows, gradients).

4. Incorporating Text

Add text to convey your message:

  • Select the Text Tool and choose a font that fits your theme.
  • Use text effects such as warping or layer styles to enhance visibility.
  • Consider text hierarchy for important information (e.g., title, date, location).

Final Touches and Filters

5. Applying Filters for Effects

Enhance your poster design by applying filters:

  • Select layers you want to apply effects to.
  • Go to the Filter menu and experiment with options like Blur, Noise, or Stylize.

6. Reviewing and Adjusting

Take a step back and review your design:

  • Ensure text is legible and stands out against the background.
  • Adjust colors, shapes, and text as necessary for balance and harmony.
  • Consider feedback from peers for further improvements.
