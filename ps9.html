Lesson Overview

This lesson introduces adjustment layers in Photoshop, focusing on powerful tools like Curves, Exposure, and other adjustments to enhance image quality non-destructively.

Adjustment Layers

1. What Are Adjustment Layers?

Adjustment layers allow you to apply color and tonal adjustments to your image without permanently changing the pixel values. They offer a non-destructive way to edit and experiment with your images.

2. Common Adjustment Layers

  • Curves: Adjusts the brightness and contrast of an image. Curves allow you to control tonal range by manipulating anchor points on the curve graph.
  • Exposure: Controls the overall exposure of an image. You can use this tool to adjust brightness and control highlights, midtones, and shadows.
  • Hue/Saturation: Alters the hue, saturation, and lightness of colors in your image. It’s great for color adjustments and correcting color casts.
  • Brightness/Contrast: A basic tool for adjusting the brightness and contrast levels of an image.
  • Levels: Adjusts the black, midtone, and white points of an image to enhance tonal range.
  • Color Balance: Allows fine-tuning of the colors in your image, particularly useful for balancing shadows, midtones, and highlights.

3. Using the Curves Adjustment

To use the Curves adjustment layer effectively, follow these steps:

  1. Go to Layer > New Adjustment Layer > Curves.
  2. A graph will appear with a line from bottom left to top right. This represents your image’s tonal range from shadows to highlights.
  3. Click on the line to add anchor points, then drag them up or down to adjust the tones. Moving the line up increases brightness, while moving it down darkens tones.
  4. Experiment with anchor points for precise adjustments.

4. Using the Exposure Adjustment

To improve an image's exposure, follow these steps:

  1. Select Layer > New Adjustment Layer > Exposure.
  2. Adjust the Exposure slider for overall brightness, the Offset slider for shadow tones, and the Gamma Correction slider for midtones.
  3. Use small adjustments to maintain image quality.

Practical Exercises

5. Exercise: Enhancing Image Contrast with Curves

Use the Curves adjustment layer to enhance the contrast in an image.

  1. Open an image in Photoshop.
  2. Create a Curves adjustment layer.
  3. Add anchor points to the curve and adjust to boost contrast in the shadows and highlights.

6. Exercise: Correcting Exposure Issues

Correct an underexposed or overexposed image using the Exposure adjustment layer.

  1. Open an image with exposure issues in Photoshop.
  2. Create an Exposure adjustment layer.
  3. Adjust the Exposure and Gamma Correction sliders to balance the image brightness.

7. Tips for Using Adjustment Layers

  • Non-Destructive Edits: Adjustment layers keep the original image intact. You can always edit or delete these layers without harming the image.
  • Layer Masks: Apply masks to adjustment layers to restrict the effect to specific parts of the image.
  • Use Layer Opacity: Adjust the opacity of adjustment layers to fine-tune the effect strength.
  • Blend Modes: Experiment with blend modes in the Layers panel to achieve unique effects with adjustment layers.
