Course Recap

Throughout this course, we have covered a variety of essential tools and techniques in Photoshop. This recap will reinforce your understanding of these tools and how to apply them in your projects.

Recap of Tools

1. Basic Tools Overview

We explored fundamental tools that form the backbone of your workflow:

  • Move Tool: Essential for positioning layers and elements.
  • Marquee Tool: Used for making rectangular and elliptical selections.
  • Lasso Tool: Allows for freehand selections.
  • Quick Selection Tool: Quickly selects an area based on color and texture.
  • Magic Wand Tool: Selects areas of similar color.
  • Brush Tool: Used for painting and retouching.

2. Selections and Layers

Understanding selections and layers is crucial:

  • Selections allow you to isolate specific areas for editing.
  • Layers enable you to work non-destructively, keeping your original images intact.
  • Layer masks allow for flexible blending and adjustments.

Final Project Brief

3. Project Overview

For your final project, you will create a comprehensive piece that incorporates the techniques and tools learned throughout the course. Your project should showcase your skills in:

  • Image selection and compositing.
  • Color correction and adjustment layers.
  • Layer management and blending modes.
  • Text and typography integration.

4. Submission Guidelines

Please adhere to the following guidelines when submitting your project:

  • Ensure your project is saved as a PSD file with all layers intact.
  • Export a high-resolution JPEG or PNG version of your final image.
  • Include a brief description of your creative process and the tools you used.
