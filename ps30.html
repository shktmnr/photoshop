Project: Complex Composite Image

Course Catalog Day-wise Breakdown

Project Objectives

This project involves creating a complex composite image using various techniques learned throughout the course. You will combine multiple images, apply adjustments, and use advanced blending techniques to create a cohesive artwork.

Getting Started

1. Prepare Your Images

Select and gather all the images you want to use in your composite. Ensure they are high-quality and relevant to the theme of your project.

2. Create a New Document

Open Photoshop and create a new document:

  1. Go to File > New.
  2. Set your desired dimensions and resolution (e.g., 1920x1080 pixels at 300 DPI).
  3. Click Create.

Building Your Composite

3. Importing Images

Drag and drop your images into the new document or use File > Place Embedded to insert them.

4. Arrange and Resize Layers

Use the Transform Tool (Ctrl + T) to resize and position each image layer as needed. Ensure they are correctly aligned and arranged to create a balanced composition.

Refining Your Composite

5. Use Layer Masks

Apply layer masks to blend images seamlessly:

  1. Select a layer and click the Add Layer Mask icon at the bottom of the Layers panel.
  2. Use a soft brush with black to paint over areas you want to hide or blend.
  3. Switch to white to reveal areas as needed.

6. Adjusting Colors and Lighting

To achieve a cohesive look:

  • Use Adjustment Layers for color correction (e.g., Curves, Levels).
  • Adjust brightness and contrast to match the images.

Final Touches

7. Add Effects and Filters

Enhance your composite with effects:

  • Consider applying a subtle blur or noise effect to unify textures.
  • Experiment with Layer Styles for shadows or glows.

8. Review and Export

Before exporting:

  • Zoom in and out to check details and overall composition.
  • Save your project as a PSD file to preserve layers.
  • Export your final image via File > Export > Export As for web or print use.

Conclusion

9. Tips for a Successful Composite
